Search Engines

There are two types of search engines, crawler-based or human-based, that regulate the way individuals receive relevant information. A crawler-based search engine is automatic and searches through meta tags and titles to determine the relevance and accounts for popularity of the website to order and list results. Human-based search engine utilize human references to order relevance of websites through meta tags, titles, and human review.

Importance of Keywords in Meta Tags

Keywords and description are vital for the crawler to understand what the webpage content entails. Keywords should be natural to the content of the page and should not be repetitive to draw the attention of the crawler. Description in meta tags allows the crawler to present a brief description of the webpage or content related to the search.

Four Elements for Writing Content Links

Links are a vital asset to the success of a website and its SEO. The more links that are relevant to the content, the more traffic received and sent off the website, allows the crawlers to tally popularity points for the website. There are four basic elements to creating relevant content links: Easy to use, navigate and understand; Provide direct, actionable information relevant to the query; Professionally designed and accessible to modern browsers; and deliver high quality, legitimate, credible content. Each four of these elements or principles sets the website to draw the most attention and clicks to a website, in return driving up views helping to show credibility and popularity to the crawlers for better search placement.

Good Hierarchy

Good heading hierarchy consists of using bigger sized fonts for the different layers of the webpage. A page title should always be greater than its category, which will be greater than the subcategory and so forth. This helps direct the viewers around the page and shows importance of information. This can be done using the h1-h6 heading tags for html. The following menu displays a great example of proper heading hierarchy:
